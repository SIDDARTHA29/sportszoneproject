Sports Overview

Cricket Overview

Cricket is a popular sport played between two teams, typically made up of 11 players each. The game is played on a circular or oval-shaped field with a 22-yard pitch in the center. One team bats while the other bowls and fields. The goal is to score as many runs as possible while trying to dismiss the opposing team.

The main forms of cricket are Test cricket, One-Day Internationals (ODIs), and Twenty20 (T20) cricket, each with different formats and rules. Cricket has a rich history, with countries like India, Australia, England, and Pakistan contributing significantly to the sport's development.

Basketball Overview

Table Tennis Overview

Table tennis, also known as ping pong, is a sport in which two or four players hit a lightweight ball back and forth across a table using small solid rackets. The game takes place on a rectangular table with a net stretched across the middle.

Table tennis is a fast-paced sport that requires quick reflexes, precise hand-eye coordination, and the ability to strategically place the ball on the table to outmaneuver your opponent.

Badminton Overview

Badminton is a racquet sport played by either two players (singles) or two teams of two players each (doubles). The object of the game is to hit a shuttlecock over a net so that it lands in the opponent's half of the court.

Badminton is an Olympic sport, known for its fast-paced gameplay, which requires excellent agility, speed, and tactical thinking. It is particularly popular in countries such as China, Indonesia, and India.

Kabaddi Overview

Kabaddi is a contact team sport that originated in India. The game is played between two teams, each taking turns to send a "raider" into the opponent's side of the court to tag members of the opposing team while holding their breath and chanting "kabaddi."

Kabaddi requires both physical strength and strategic planning. The sport is extremely popular in India and has international leagues like the Pro Kabaddi League.
